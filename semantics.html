Semantics is fancy wizard-speak for "meaning." When we talk about semantic HTML, we're talking about using elements that actually describe what they contain, rather than just how they look. It's like calling a unicorn a "unicorn" instead of a "pointy horse with sparkles."

âœ¨ Why This Matters

You might wonder, "Why not just use divs with classes?" Great question, curious apprentice! Here's why semantic elements are magical:

  1. Accessibility: Screen readers can navigate semantic elements more easily, helping visually impaired unicorn enthusiasts enjoy your content.
  2. SEO: Search engines understand the structure of your content better, like unicorns can smell carrots from miles away.
  3. Maintainability: Future developers (including future you) can understand your code more easily.
  4. Future-proofing: As browsers and assistive technologies evolve, semantic HTML will become even more powerful.
